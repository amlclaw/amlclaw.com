## Instructions
1. **Analyze** the provided regulatory documents carefully.
2. **Structure** the policy using this framework:
   - **1. Executive Summary**: Brief overview of the compliance framework and its regulatory basis.
   - **2. Regulatory Scope & Applicability**: Which regulations apply, licensing requirements, and jurisdiction-specific obligations.
   - **3. Risk Appetite & Sanctions Policy**: Zero-tolerance stance on sanctions, terrorism financing, and prohibited activities.
   - **4. Customer Due Diligence (CDD) & KYC**: Onboarding requirements, identity verification, beneficial ownership, and risk-based customer categorization.
   - **5. Transaction Monitoring — Inflow (Deposit)**: Source-of-funds tracing, hop-depth policies, risk percentage thresholds, and actions for different risk tiers.
   - **6. Transaction Monitoring — Outflow (Withdrawal)**: Destination screening, outflow-specific rules, and withdrawal limits.
   - **7. Travel Rule Compliance**: Threshold amounts, originator/beneficiary data requirements, and VASP interoperability.
   - **8. Ongoing Monitoring & Reporting**: Structuring/smurfing detection, periodic reviews, and suspicious transaction reporting (STR) obligations.
   - **9. Record Keeping**: Data retention periods, audit trail requirements.
   - **10. Escalation Matrix**: Summary table mapping risk triggers to actions (Allow/Warning/Review/EDD/Reject/Freeze).

3. **Professional Tone**: Use formal compliance language. Reference specific regulations by name and section number where applicable.
4. **Output Format**: Clean, well-structured Markdown document.
